1. Prologue: The Silent War

In the heart of a holy land divided by belief, whispers of forgotten truths stir the air. A war of ideologies brews beneath the silence.

2. Sacred Bloodlines

Two ancient tribes—descendants of prophets—cling to relics and texts that claim divine favor. Their history is soaked in both miracles and massacre.

3. The Caravan Massacre

When a peaceful Saracen caravan is slaughtered by crusaders, tension reignites. Vengeance becomes sacred. Peace becomes a prayer unanswered.

4. The Scholar's Secret

Hidden scrolls discovered by a monk unveil a prophecy—one that speaks not of war, but unity. Yet both sides fear it.

5. The Desert Pact

A forbidden meeting in the dunes brings together a Saracen general and a disillusioned Templar. They see the same light in different skies.

6. Betrayal in the Temple

Holy ground is defiled not by outsiders, but by those sworn to protect it. Power intoxicates the clergy. Truth is buried deeper.

7. The Crimson Crescent

A resistance rises under the banner of the Crimson Crescent—uniting outcasts from both factions. They fight not for faith, but for freedom.

8. Scrolls of Fire

The stolen prophecy is revealed to the world. Riots erupt. Cities burn. The idea of peace becomes the most dangerous weapon.

9. The Oracle's Vision

A blind oracle speaks of a child born under twin moons—destined to end the conflict. Both sides race to find or destroy the child.

10. Siege of the Golden Gate

A final stand is made at the ancient city gates. Neither army knows they march toward the same truth... nor the same lie.

11. Ashes of the Faithful

The temple burns. Icons melt. Still, the people kneel—not in fear, but in mourning. The gods remain silent.

12. Echoes of the Martyrs

Stories of rebels become psalms. Children sing of warriors who bled for no banner but hope. The legend grows.

13. The Last Sermon

One voice, broken but clear, delivers a final plea in the ruins. “We were not meant to conquer, but to coexist.”

14. The Pact Reborn

From the rubble, survivors form a new covenant—one not written in stone, but in shared suffering. A fragile beginning.

15. Epilogue: The New Scroll

A single parchment, written in both tongues, is placed at the altar. No title. Just a question: “What if we listened?”
